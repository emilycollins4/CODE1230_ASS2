Find Me a Rich Man

A data analysis showing where the wealthiest young men between the ages of 25 and 34 are situated in Sydney, as per the 2016 census.

About

Through observing the 2016 data, it is possible to find the most eligible (and wealthiest) boyfriend - utilising QGIS maps to exactly pin point where the largest number of men aged 25-34 live and exactly how much money they make weekly.

Process

Joining the data set to a map of NSW and setting the variable to what we wish to find - in this case, rich boys.

More Data

Through analysing further data such as marrital status and highest level of education, we are able to find the most eligible bachelor.

Outcome

With all of this important information, a layered map will be made - pin pointing the areas in Sydney where rich, smart and single men can be found.

Final Goal

What can be done with this information? The world is now your oyster - go and find your man.

Mesh Blocks

Mesh blocks are the smallest geographical area defined by the Australian Bureau of Statistics - all other statistical areas or regions are built up from mesh blocks. They are able to identify land use. The 2016 ASGS contains 358,122 mesh blocks covering the whole of Australia without gaps or overlaps. (ASGS, 2016)

High Weekly Income


Through the observation of the 2016 data set showing the weekly income of males between the ages of 25 and 35 in Sydney, Mesh blocks are colored accordingly to show income levels. The lighter blue areas have lower weekly income and the darker purple areas are the highest income areas. Areas in the Eastern Suburbs as well as in the City have higher weekly income rates, as well as in industrial areas due to a higher population of men and higher paying jobs. Expensive areas in the North Shore and around beaches are mostly light purple or blue not because of a lower income but because they are largely populated by families and retireees. As shown in the second map, the Latte line runs down through the middle of Northern Sydney and through to the Eastern suburbs which doesn't follow a specific pattern but does enclose the wealthier areas of Sydney and the main universities and industrial workplaces which makes sense as they would be heavily populated with young men.
